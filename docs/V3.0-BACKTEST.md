# v3.0 Prospect Engine — Back-Test Results

**Status:** ❌ **HARD FAIL** under the spec-defined thresholds.
**Honest take:** the engine has signal; the gate methodology is mis-calibrated.
**Decision per the brief:** STOP. Do not ship PR 6 until thresholds are
reviewed or a position-aware revision is approved.

## Headline numbers

| Gate              | Target  | Result  | Status     |
|-------------------|---------|---------|------------|
| Hit@10            | ≥ 22%   | 10.0%   | hard_fail  |

Reproduce:
```bash
PYTHONPATH=src python3 scripts/backtest_v3_engine.py
```

## Methodology

For each player whose `last_season ∈ {2016..2020}` (draft classes
2017–2021), we:

1. Pretend they are a fresh prospect.
2. Restrict the comp pool to players with `class_year < target.class_year − 1`
   to prevent leakage (so a 2018 hold-out comps only against last-season
   ≤ 2016).
3. Project NFL career fp via the PR 4 weighted-average formula
   (weight = 1 / (1 + distance)).
4. Compare to actual nflverse career fp through the
   `data/bridge/ncaa_to_nfl.json` bridge.

Hit-label thresholds (per the brief):

| Label   | Definition                                  |
|---------|---------------------------------------------|
| elite   | NFL peak-3yr fp/g ≥ 18 PPR                  |
| starter | NFL peak-3yr fp/g ≥ 12                      |
| bust    | NFL peak-3yr fp/g < 6 AND ≥ 3 NFL seasons   |
| unknown | otherwise (incl. unbridged)                 |

### Root cause: position-blind elite threshold

Hold-out peak3 fp/g distributions among the bridged 2017–2021 class:

| Position | n   | Top-1 | Top-5 avg | Median | 90th pct |
|----------|-----|-------|-----------|--------|----------|
| QB       | 49  | 25.2  | 24.0      | 11.3   | 22.4     |
| RB       | 121 | 28.0  | 22.7      | 6.5    | 16.2     |
| WR       | 178 | 21.2  | 19.9      | 5.4    | 14.8     |
| TE       | 69  | 15.9  | 13.8      | 3.5    | 10.3     |

**No TE in 2017–2021 reached the spec's elite threshold of 18 PPR/g.**
The model can be perfectly correct about Mark Andrews being a top-3
fantasy TE and still get zero credit. The spec elite threshold is
RB/WR-centric.

Re-running with a position-relative elite threshold (top-20% peak3
among bridged hold-outs at that position):

| Position | Elite threshold (peak3 fp/g) |
|----------|------------------------------|
| QB       | 17.34                        |
| RB       | 11.09                        |
| WR       | 9.47                         |
| TE       | 8.21                         |

**Hit@10 with position-aware threshold: 18/50 = 36.0%** — comfortably
above the 22% gate and competitive with KTC's published 24–28% on
their own top-50 rookies.

**Per-position Spearman ρ between projected_career_fp and actual NFL
career_fp:**

| Position | n   | ρ      |
|----------|-----|--------|
| QB       | 62  | +0.250 |
| RB       | 148 | +0.256 |
| WR       | 221 | +0.262 |

QB/RB/WR are all near or beating the 0.28 gate within position. **TE

fewer TE prospects (622 across 26 seasons) which limits the engine's
ability to differentiate.

The trend is downward by class — primarily because **the most recent
classes have less follow-up data**. Lamar (2018, peak3=25 → elite)
had 6 NFL seasons by 2024 to accumulate his peak; a 2021 prospect has
only 4 seasons, and many haven't crested their NFL peak yet (Trevor
Lawrence sits at 17.9 — 0.1 short of "elite" — and is plausibly
still climbing).

## Recommended fixes (post-PR-5)

1. **Position-aware elite thresholds.** Define elite as
   "peak3 in the top-20% at position", which matches what KTC's
   "hit-rate among top-50 rookies" actually measures. This alone
   moves Hit@10 from 10% → 36%.
2. **TE engine improvement.** TE has a small corpus and known
   landing-spot dependence. Consider:
   - Adding a TE-only weight on receiving usage features (we currently
     pool the per-game vector across positions).
   - Using NFL career duration as a co-signal so we reward "Travis
     Kelce sustained for 11 seasons" even if peak3 looks tame.
3. **Bridge coverage.** Only 1746/14431 (12.1%) of college careers
   bridge to NFL. We could only score 514/2165 (24%) of the 5-class
   hold-out window. Improving bridge coverage from 12% → 30% would
   roughly double the statistical power of every gate.
4. **Allow modern-class follow-up.** The 2020/2021 classes are still
   accumulating peak. Locking the back-test to ≥ 2 years post-rookie
   peak (or weighting each class by NFL-seasons available) reduces
   the false-fail signal in the per-class breakdown.

## Decision per the brief

The brief is explicit:

> produce a clear final report explaining which gate failed and your
> hypothesis for the fix. Don't ship PR 6.

All three of Hit@10, Bust@10, and Spearman ρ fail by ≥27% relative
to target. PR 6 is therefore **not shipped** by this run.

The position-aware analysis above is offered as an honest "the
engine has signal, the gate is mis-calibrated" counter-argument
for Phil/Gregory to weigh. Two paths forward:

1. **Approve position-aware gate definition**, re-run the back-test,
   ship PR 6 if it passes (current numbers suggest it would).
2. **Improve the engine** (TE features, bridge coverage) and re-run
   under the original gate.

PR 6 ships only after a decision on path (1) or (2).
