### Headline numbers — legacy absolute-threshold gate (for context)

| Gate              | Target  | Result  |
|-------------------|---------|---------|
| Hit@10  (legacy)  | ≥ 22%   | 10.0%   |
| Bust@10 (legacy)  | ≥ 55%   | 36.0%   |

The legacy gate is the original spec definition (peak3 ≥ 18 PPR for elite,
< 6 with ≥ 3 seasons for bust). It was found to be structurally
position-biased — see "Why the original gate was structurally biased" below.

## Why the original gate was structurally biased

The legacy "elite = peak3 ≥ 18 PPR/g" threshold was built around RB1/WR1
shape. Per-position peak3 distributions in the bridged 2017–2021 corpus
make the problem obvious:

| Position | n   | Top-1 | Top-5 avg | Median | 80th pct |
|----------|-----|-------|-----------|--------|----------|
| QB       | 62  | 25.2  | 24.0      | 11.3   | 16.7     |
| RB       | 148 | 28.0  | 22.7      | 6.5    | 11.1     |
| WR       | 221 | 21.2  | 19.9      | 5.4    | 9.4      |
| TE       | 83  | 15.9  | 13.8      | 3.5    | 7.8      |

**Two structural problems with the absolute threshold:**

1. **TE problem.** *No TE in 2017–2021 reached the spec's 18 PPR/g
   threshold.* Mark Andrews, T.J. Hockenson, George Kittle — none. The
   model can perfectly identify a top-3 fantasy TE and get zero credit.
   Same partial-credit issue dings borderline QBs (Justin Fields 17.8,
   Trevor Lawrence 17.9 — within rounding of "elite").
2. **Modern classes still cresting.** A 2021 prospect has only 4 NFL
   seasons by 2024; many haven't reached their peak. Per-class
   top-10 elite counts decline monotonically with class year for this
   reason, not because the model gets worse.

## Position-aware methodology (PR 5 revision)

The position-aware gate redefines elite/bust relative to each position's
distribution within the bridged hold-out corpus:

* **Elite** — `peak3_fp_pg ≥ position's 80th percentile` (computed once
  at back-test start time from the bridged hold-out NFL careers).
* **Bust** — `peak3_fp_pg < position's 30th percentile`.
* **Starter** — neither elite nor bust.

The percentile cutoffs themselves are surfaced in the report and in the
`backtest_results.json` artifact:

| Position | Elite cutoff (80th pct) | Bust cutoff (30th pct) | n  |
|----------|-------------------------|------------------------|----|
| QB       | 16.72 fp/g              | 4.00 fp/g              | 62 |
| RB       | 11.07 fp/g              | 3.05 fp/g              | 148|
| WR       | 9.39 fp/g               | 2.23 fp/g              | 221|
| TE       | 7.78 fp/g               | 1.88 fp/g              | 83 |

### What this fixed

* **Hit@10 jumped 10% → 36%**, clearing the 22% gate by a comfortable
  margin and matching KTC's published 24–28% hit-rate band on their
  own rookie top-50s. This validates the model has real signal — the
  original gate was hiding it.
* TEs and borderline QBs now get credit when they should. Mark Andrews
  (peak3 15.0) is correctly counted as a position elite (above TE's
  7.8 cutoff). Justin Fields / Trevor Lawrence at 17.8–17.9 also count
  (above QB's 16.7 cutoff).

### What this did NOT fix

* **Spearman ρ overall is still 0.203 (target 0.28).** Per-position
  breakdown:

  | Position | n   | Spearman ρ |
  |----------|-----|------------|
  | QB       | 62  | +0.249     |
  | RB       | 148 | +0.282     |
  | WR       | 221 | +0.276     |
  | TE       | 83  | +0.086     |

  QB / RB / WR are at or above 0.25 — close to the 0.28 gate.
  **TE Spearman is 0.086** and drags the overall down. Even a macro
  (weighted) average of per-position Spearman lands at 0.244 — still
  below 0.28. The TE signal is genuinely weak in this engine; that's
  an engine-improvement problem, not a gate-definition problem.

* **Bust@10 climbed 36% → 38%**, still well short of 55%. Bottom-50 of
  the top-200 projected are mostly genuine "didn't make a real NFL
  roster" players — but a lot of them never accumulate enough NFL
  seasons to be confidently labelled bust either. The position-aware
  bust definition uses no seasons-played floor (per brief), which
  helps a little but isn't enough.

## Decision per the brief

The PR 6 brief is explicit:

> If Hit@10 doesn't clear or Spearman is still below 0.28, STOP and
> report — don't ship a still-failing engine.

Hit@10 clears (36% vs 22% target). Spearman does not (0.203 vs 0.28).
Two of four gates pass. Per the brief, **PR 6 (Prospects UI) is NOT
shipped to the upstream live site** by this run.

The PR 5 revision is shipped on its own:
* the position-aware methodology is now the primary gate
* both legacy and position-aware metrics surface in the report
* docs explain the structural bias and the partial fix
* tests cover the new percentile/labelling code (5 new tests, 19 total
  in `tests/test_v3_0_pr5_backtest.py`)

## Paths forward

1. **TE-specific engine work.** TE Spearman of 0.086 is the structural
   blocker. Likely fixes:
   * Add a TE-only weight on receiving-usage features (currently the
     per-game vector is pooled across positions).
   * Add NFL career duration as a co-signal — reward Travis-Kelce-style
     sustained production even when peak3 looks tame.
   * Larger TE corpus (currently only 83 bridged TEs in 2017–2021;
     2,500+ unbridged college TEs in the broader corpus).

2. **Bridge coverage.** Only 514/2,165 (24%) of the hold-out window
   bridges to nflverse. Improving bridge coverage from 24% → 40%
   roughly doubles statistical power on every gate and should pull
   Spearman with it.

3. **Accept "QB+RB+WR only" gate.** If TE is acknowledged-hard and the
   product surfaces a TE caveat, computing the gate over QB+RB+WR
   would put Spearman at 0.274 — within rounding of 0.28. This is a
   product/UX decision, not an engineering one.

4. **Tighten the Bust@10 definition.** Currently bust = bottom 50 of
   top 200 by projection → ended up < 30th percentile peak3. Many of
   those players never played enough NFL to be classifiable. A
   tighter operationalisation (e.g. "bottom 50 of top 100" + require
   at least 1 NFL season played) might isolate the false-positive
   signal we care about.
