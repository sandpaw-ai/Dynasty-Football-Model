## The current filter chain

`load_corpus()` returns ~all skill-position PlayerCareer rows. The pool reduction happens in two places:

**1. `PlayerCareer.is_long_arc()`** (similarity_v1.py:217) returns True iff one of:
- `is_retired(through=2022)` — i.e. `last_season ≤ 2022`
- `len(seasons) ≥ 8` (LONG_ARC_MIN_SEASONS)
- `last_age ≥ 33` AND `len(seasons) ≥ 6` (LONG_ARC_VETERAN_*)

**2. The corpus-building loop in `run_engine`** (similarity_v1.py:997-1010) requires `≥2 seasons` AND `is_long_arc()`.

So a player who:
- has ≥ 2 NFL seasons,
- was last active in 2023 or later,
- did not reach 8 seasons,
- did not reach age 33 with 6 seasons,

is silently dropped from `long_arc_arcs`. Their fantasy-point arc is never built; they never appear as a comp candidate; they never inject into anybody's projected outcome distribution.

### Why short-career-retired-pre-2022 players ARE in the pool (and why that masked the bug)

Tebow, Ridder, Christian Ponder, EJ Manuel, Aaron Brooks etc. retired by 2022 → `is_retired(through=2022)` is True → they pass `is_long_arc`. Phil's v2.3.3 instruction ("comping to Tebow should drag you down via the survival multiplier") relied on these older busts being present. They are. But the comp pool stops accumulating busts as the dataset rolls forward — every wash-out from 2023 onward gets excluded until 2028+ when their `is_retired(through=2022)` would shift.

**The pool ages out of new busts.** That is the survivorship bias.

## The fix: career-stage-matched comp pool

Two strategies are on the table (per the assignment brief). I'm implementing the recommended hybrid: **Strategy 2 with a min-floor**.

### Design

1. **Broaden the corpus.** Drop the `is_long_arc()` gate at corpus build time. Build a fantasy-point arc for every skill-position career with `≥ 2` completed seasons (already era-pace adjusted). Tag each comp arc with `n_completed_seasons` (max season age in its arc, basically — but simpler: `len(seasons)` for retireds, `len(seasons-trimmed-to-current_season)` for actives).
2. **Career-stage-matched comp pool.** In `find_comps(target, target_age)`, compute `target_n_seasons` (number of completed seasons the target has played through `current_season`). Filter the comp pool to candidates with `n_completed_seasons ≥ max(target_n_seasons, FLOOR_SEASONS)` where `FLOOR_SEASONS = 3`. This ensures:
   - A current player with N completed seasons is compared against historical players who have ALSO crossed N seasons (so the comp has a real post-N arc to project from).
   - For very-early-career targets (N ≤ 3), the floor of 3 prevents comping against 1-season noise.
   - For veterans (Mahomes N=9), the gate becomes ≥9, ≈ the existing 8-season long-arc gate — their pool is approximately unchanged.
3. **Surfacing bust outcomes is automatic.** The 715 short-career players (mostly 2-7 seasons) now slot into the comp pools of current players whose N matches. A 3rd-year Stroud will see Marcus Mariota (5 NFL seasons), Mitch Trubisky (~7), Dwayne Haskins (3), Trevor Siemian (4) as candidates. The post-age-21 / post-stage-3 projection for these comps reflects their actual realized fp from that age forward — which for busts is small or zero. The comp_weighted_fp distribution now contains realistic bust anchors.

### What this does to the survival_multiplier

The existing `compute_survival` (v2.2/2.3.3) measures the share of a target's comps that washed out. Today: the comp pool is hand-filtered for survivors, so the survival multiplier under-fires for young targets. Post-fix: the comp pool contains the realistic bust mix, so `compute_survival` becomes a more honest filter. This is a feature, not a regression — Phil specifically calls out that the survival mechanism is supposed to bite when bust-comps are present.

### What this does NOT change

- `percentile_table` (career-stage percentile bands) is still built from long-arc players. The percentile bins are reference anchors ("what does a 95th-percentile age-26 WR look like?") — they should remain based on the high-information long-arc set. Bust anchors don't help percentile shape.
- The proven-production floor logic from v3.1 (recent-3yr / 2yr / 1yr targets) is untouched.
- Era-pace adjustment is untouched.
- The rookie-NFL-fp-arc engine (rookies with 0-1 NFL seasons) is untouched — it has its own corpus.

## Expected impact

- **Stroud (3 NFL seasons):** comp pool size grows substantially (+50% or more vs v3.1). Top-25 comps now include short-career flame-outs. `production_score` drops 15-30%.
- **Anthony Richardson (similar career stage):** same effect, mobile-QB-specific bust comps appear.
- **Caleb Williams / rookies (1 NFL season):** projected mostly via `rookie_nfl_fp_arc` engine — unaffected by this change. If he's routed through `arc_project_player`, his pool gate is the FLOOR_SEASONS=3 minimum.
- **Mahomes/Allen/Lamar/Burrow/Dak:** gate ≥ 9 ≈ unchanged from 8+, comp pool nearly identical. Rank movement ≤ ±3.
- **Justin Jefferson (6 seasons):** gate ≥ 6 — was already 8 (so this strictly broadens). Some new bust comps possible. Expected ±5 max.
