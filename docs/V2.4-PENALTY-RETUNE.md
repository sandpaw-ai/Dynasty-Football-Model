# v2.4 — Penalty Stack Retune

**Branch:** `ada/v2.4-pr3-era-pace-retune`
**Audited:** 2026-05-23
**Corpus:** unified 1980-2025 (54,405 stitched rows = 49,514 post-1999 + 4,891 pre-1999)

## Scope

The v2.2 survival / confidence / late-breakout stack and the v2.3.3 bust amplifier were calibrated against the 1999+-only corpus. With v2.4 adding ~150 RB careers and ~100 WR careers to the retired pool, the bust-rate baselines shift downward (more players had long, productive careers in the broader pool).

This doc:

1. Recomputes the per-position bust-rate baselines from the unified corpus.
2. Audits `SHORT_CAREER_MAX_SEASONS = 5` and `SURVIVAL_BUST_MAX_SEASONS = 8` against the unified-corpus distributions.
3. Documents the 0.9× pre-1999 confidence haircut implementation.

## 1. Per-position bust-rate baselines (recomputed)

Method: count retired (last_season ≤ `RETIRED_THROUGH_SEASON = 2022`) careers with ≥2 seasons per position. Bust = age ≤ `SURVIVAL_BUST_AGE = 30` AND career_length < `SURVIVAL_BUST_MAX_SEASONS = 8`. Short = career_length ≤ `SHORT_CAREER_MAX_SEASONS = 5`. Durable = career_length ≥ 6.

| Position | n_retired | bust_rate (v2.3 vs v2.4) | short_rate (v2.3 vs v2.4) | durable_rate (v2.3 vs v2.4) | avg_len (v2.3 vs v2.4) |
|----------|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
| QB | 159 → 266 | **0.459 → 0.421** (-3.8pp) | **0.629 → 0.553** (-7.6pp) | **0.371 → 0.447** (+7.6pp) | 5.50 → 6.12 |
| RB | 482 → 789 | 0.778 → 0.786 (+0.8pp)   | 0.662 → 0.650 (-1.2pp)   | 0.338 → 0.350 (+1.2pp)    | 4.76 → 4.87 |
| WR | 654 → 925 | **0.726 → 0.697** (-2.9pp) | 0.654 → 0.611 (-4.3pp)   | 0.346 → 0.389 (+4.3pp)    | 4.93 → 5.31 |
| TE | 332 → 442 | 0.669 → 0.665 (-0.4pp)   | 0.602 → 0.588 (-1.4pp)   | 0.398 → 0.412 (+1.4pp)    | 5.20 → 5.33 |

**Reading:** The QB bust rate drops 3.8pp because the pre-1999 pool included a generation of long-career pocket passers (Marino, Montana, Kelly, Moon, Elway) who didn't bust by age 30. The WR bust rate drops 2.9pp because the same era had Hall-of-Fame durable WRs (Rice, Largent, Reed, Ellard). RB / TE bust rates are essentially unchanged — workhorse RBs DID still cliff at 30 in the 80s-90s (different reason than modern: usage, not coordinator decisions), and pre-1999 TE was a niche position.

**Implication for ranking:** The survival multiplier formula consumes `bust_rate` directly (not a deviation from the baseline). Changing the baselines does NOT silently move scores. The baselines are exposed as `BUST_RATE_BASELINE` and `DURABLE_CAREER_BASELINE` constants for transparency: rankings consumers can compare a player's comp-pool bust rate (e.g. Sam Howell ~0.85) against the league baseline to see whether the comp pool is structurally bust-heavy (RB baseline 0.786 is just league average) or unusually bust-heavy (Howell at 0.85 is 6pp above the QB baseline of 0.42).

## 2. Threshold audit

For `SHORT_CAREER_MAX_SEASONS = 5` and `SURVIVAL_BUST_MAX_SEASONS = 8` to remain well-calibrated, they need to bracket meaningful percentiles in the corpus distribution.

| Position | p25 | p50 | p75 | p90 | fraction ≤ 5 (short) | fraction ≥ 8 (durable+) |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|----------------------|--------------------------|
| QB | 3 | 5 | 8 | 12 | 0.553 | 0.293 |
| RB | 3 | 4 | 6 | 9 | 0.650 | 0.156 |
| WR | 3 | 4 | 7 | 10 | 0.611 | 0.231 |
| TE | 3 | 4 | 7 | 10 | 0.588 | 0.235 |

- `SHORT_CAREER_MAX_SEASONS = 5` flags ~55-65% of careers across positions — sits at the median for QB / above the median for RB/WR/TE. Honest signal: "your comp pool's median career length was a short one".
- `SURVIVAL_BUST_MAX_SEASONS = 8` lines up with the QB p75 and is comfortably above the RB p75 (6) and WR/TE p75 (7). Honest signal: "this comp had a settled, finished career that didn't reach the league's p75 length".

**Decision: no formula change.** The thresholds remain well-calibrated against the unified corpus. The v2.3.3 wash-out amplifier (top-5 bust count × 0.08 per bust, floor 0.6) also still resolves correctly against the unified-corpus distributions.

## 3. The 0.9× pre-1999 confidence haircut

**Decision source:** Phil's section 10.3 (V2.4-PRE1999-LEGENDS): *"Conservative; era-pace is principled but not perfect."*

**Implementation:** `src/dynasty/engine/fantasy_arc_similarity.py`. The haircut multiplies the similarity weight by 0.9 for any comp whose **snapshot season** (the comp's season at the snapshot age — the season we're treating as that comp's "this is the age the target is at" anchor) is strictly before 1999.

### Gating: snapshot SEASON, not player

This is the critical nuance. The haircut is about **pre-1999 data-quality uncertainty**, NOT about the player.

- ✅ Walter Payton at age 30 (1984 season) → snapshot < 1999 → haircut applied (0.9×).
- ✅ Emmitt Smith at age 26 (1995 season) → snapshot < 1999 → haircut applied (0.9×).
- ❌ Emmitt Smith at age 31 (2000 season) → snapshot ≥ 1999 → **no haircut**. A 2000-season comp is a 2000-season comp regardless of who lived through 1990-1998 to reach it.
- ❌ Brett Favre at age 32 (2001 season) → snapshot ≥ 1999 → no haircut.

This is correct because:
- Pre-1999 data has known gaps (no EPA columns, no air yards, sparse target data, fumble splits aggregated). Players at pre-1999 snapshot ages are derived from a thinner stat schema → uncertainty justifies a small haircut.
- Post-1999 data for a crossover player is the SAME schema everyone else's post-1999 data uses. Applying a haircut to Emmitt-at-31 would penalize Henry's comp pool for something that isn't actually a data quality issue — it would amount to a player-based blacklist of crossover legends.

### Implementation site

`find_comps` (in `fantasy_arc_similarity.py`) bakes the haircut into `CompMatch.similarity` at construction time. Every downstream consumer — `project_player` weighted average, `compute_survival` sim-weighted bust rate, the top-K sort — therefore sees one consistent set of haircut-adjusted weights. We surface `raw_similarity` and `pre1999_haircut_applied` on `CompMatch` for diagnostics; the engine continues to use `similarity` as the effective weight.

### Hard test

A pre-1999-snapshot comp (e.g. Dickerson 1991 in Henry's comp pool) MUST have `similarity = raw_similarity × 0.9`. A crossover post-1999-snapshot comp (e.g. Emmitt 2000 in Henry's pool) MUST have `similarity == raw_similarity` exactly. Both invariants are enforced by `tests/test_v2_4_penalty_retune.py`.

## 4. What did NOT change

- The survival multiplier formula (`(1-bust)*0.5 + (1-short)*0.2 + 0.3` with top-5 amplifier).
- The confidence shrinkage thresholds (`FULL_CONFIDENCE_STARTS = 32` for QB, `FULL_CONFIDENCE_GAMES_NON_QB = 30` for non-QBs).
- The late-breakout penalty table.
- The penalty stack composition order and floor / ceiling.
- The stale-data threshold (`RECENT_STARTER_GAMES_TWO_YEAR = 12`).

PR 3's job is to add the v2.4 machinery (empirical era-pace snapshot, 0.9× haircut, per-position bust-rate diagnostics) WITHOUT changing the formula. PR 4 flips `USE_PRE1999_CORPUS=True` and observes the score shifts at the validation step; the machinery from this PR is what makes the flip safe.
